Attrition



Attrition - is the rate at which employees leave a call center within a given period, often referred to as employee turnover. The contact center industry experiences high attrition rates, with a typical benchmark ranging from 30-45% annually. This high rate is driven by factors such as low morale, lack of recognition, emotional disconnection, burnout, poor work-life balance, and micromanagement. To combat attrition, call centers can implement strategies like fostering a positive culture, offering continuous training and career paths, encouraging open communication, supporting work-life balance through flexibility and remote options, and providing fair compensation and rewards

Factors of High Attrition:

  • Poor Work Culture : Emotional disconnection from the team, isolation, and a lack of a supportive community contribute to agents leaving.

  • Lack of Empowerment : Insufficient autonomy, micromanagement, and feeling undervalued can lead to disengagement and turnover.

  • Insufficient Development and Recognition : A lack of clear career paths, training, and rewards for good performance demotivates agents and prompts them to seek opportunities elsewhere.

  • Burnout and High Stress : The demanding nature of call center work, combined with insufficient work-life balance, can lead to burnout and the need for a new job.

  • Lack of Transparency : Unclear or unfair performance reviews, a lack of open communication, and feeling unheard can decrease morale and lead to attrition.



How Attrition is Calculated:

    Attrition % =      Number of Leavers in a Period    x100

                    Average Headcount for the Period
